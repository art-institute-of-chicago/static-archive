Bibliography


Chevreul, Michel Eugene. 1859. Chevreul on Colours: The Laws of Contrast of Colour and their Application to the Arts of Painting, Decoration of Buildings, Mosaic Work, Tapestry and Carpet Weaving, Calico Printing, Dress, Paper Staining, Printing, Illumination, Landscape, and Flower Gardening, &c. Trans. John Spanton. London: Routledge, Warnes, and Routledge.

Cikovsky, Nicolai, Jr., and Franklin Kelly. 1995. Winslow Homer. Exh. cat. National Gallery of Art, Washington, D.C./Yale University Press.

Cohn, Marjorie B . 1977. Wash and Gouache: A Study of the Development of the Materials of Watercolor. Exh. cat. Cambridge, Mass.: Center for Conservation and Technical Studies, Fogg Art Museum.

Cooke, Hereward Lester. 1961. “Winslow Homer’s Water-Color Technique.” Art Quarterly 24, 2 (Summer), 169–74.

Cooper, Helen A. 1986. Winslow Homer Watercolors. Exh. cat.National Gallery of Art, Washington, D.C./Yale University Press.

Ferber, Linda S., and Barbara Dayer Gallati. 1998. Masters of Color and Light: Homer, Sargent, and the American Watercolor Movement. Exh. cat. Brooklyn Museum of Art/Smithsonian Institution Press.

Gettens, Rutherford J., and George L. Stout. 1966. Painting Materials: A Short Encyclopedia. New York: Dover Publications.

Labarre, E.J. 1937. A Dictionary of Paper and Paper-Making Terms. Amsterdam: N.V. Swets and Zeitlinger.

Mayer, Ralph. 1975. A Dictionary of Art Terms and Techniques. New York: Thomas Y. Crowell and Company.

Museum of Fine Arts, Boston. CAMEO: Conservation and Art Material Encyclopedia Online. http://cameo.mfa.org/index.asp.

Penley, Aaron Edwin. 1874. The English School of Painting in Water-Colours: Its Theory and Practice. London: A. Tarrant.

Penley, Aaron Edwin. 1883. A System of Water Colour Painting. London: Winsor and Newton.

Reed, Sue Welsh, and Carol Troyen. 1993. Awash in Color: Homer, Sargent, and the Great American Watercolor. Exh. cat. Museum of Fine Arts, Boston/Bulfinch Press/Little, Brown.

Tedeschi, Martha, and Kristi Dahm. 2008. Watercolors by Winslow Homer: The Color of Light. Exh. cat. The Art Institute of Chicago/Yale University Press.

Walsh, Judith. 1987. “Observations on the Watercolor Techniques of Homer and Sargent.” In Susan E. Strickler, ed. 1987. American Traditions in Watercolor: The Worcester Art Museum Collection, 1st ed. Worcester Art Museum/Abbeville Press, 45–65.

Walsh, Judith. 1993. “A Summer’s Pleasure: Incoming Tide, Scarborough, Maine by Winslow Homer.” American Art Journal 25, 1/2, 62–73.

Walsh, Judith. 1995. “Innovation in Homer’s Late Watercolors.” In Cikovsky and Kelly 1995, 283–300.

Winsor, William, and H. C. Newton. 1855. Hand-Book of Water-Colours: A Brief Treatise on Their Qualities and Effects when Employed in Painting; with Some Account of the General Nature of Colours, 3rd ed. London: Tilt and Bogue.
