THE AMERICA WINDOWS

America Windows was installed in the Art Institute in early May 1977, under the supervision of Charles Marq and his atelier. It was presented with much fanfare at a formal unveiling, hosted by the Auxiliary Board of the Art Institute, on May 15. Because of his admiration for Chicago and its strong commitment to public art during the 1960s and 1970s, Chagall chose to dedicate his work to Mayor Richard J. Daley, a great supporter of public art projects in the city, who had recently passed away. In the following weeks and months, numerous feature stories and press announcements praised the radiance and beauty of Chagall's achievement.

Over the years, America Windows has become one of the most beloved works in the museum's encyclopedic collection.
