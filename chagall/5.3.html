CONSERVATION of the Windows

Before treatment could begin, it was essential to determine how the windows were made, since very little specific information had been documented about the composition of the windows at the time of their installation. Certain types of glass and glass paints can be fragile, so conservation methods must be tailored to each material. Analysis using x-ray fluorescence identified a range of colorants—such as cobalt for blue, and chromium and copper for green—of the flashed glass and confirmed that the grisaille contained metal oxides. Close examination under magnification confirmed that the grisaille was well fired and showed no signs of flaking since its application.
