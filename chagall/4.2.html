CHAGALL AND MARQ Making America Windows

The process of translating Chagall's final gouache maquette into America Windows began with careful study. Charles Marq considered the color, line, and composition of the artist's design in order to determine the essential structure of the panels. Using a pen and transparent paper over the design, he indicated the lines for the caming (a framework of lead grooved bars that hold individual panes of glass together) of the windows, which would act as a guide for the studio to follow in the coming weeks.
