CHAGALL and Chicago

Chagall first conceived his proposal for The Four Seasons mosaic as a gouache drawing, which was later sent to his collaborators, master mosaicists Lino Melano and Michel Tharin, to translate as full-scale panels in their studio in Biot, France. Over the course of two years, the artist visited the Biot studio to review progress and make revisions to colors and forms. Tharin came to Chicago in the summer of 1974 to oversee the installation of the mosaic panels and then worked with Chagall on the final adjustments, including modifications to the city's skyline, which had changed since his last visit in 1958.
