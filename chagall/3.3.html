THE AMERICA WINDOWS

Chagall's evolving thoughts on the compositions of each of the panels of America Windows can be charted through his various preparatory pencil and gouache studies and sketches, as well as the final gouache maquette of 1976. He experimented with many figural and compositional elements in early studies and then, using different tones of tissue paper, tested the balance and play of color in a preparatory maquette. Finally, the artist brought the earlier studies of composition and color together in the final gouache maquette, which served as a guide for the artisans in Marq's stained-glass atelier.
