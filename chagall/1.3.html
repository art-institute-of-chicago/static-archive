CHAGALL and Stained Glass

Chagall's explorations also included the centuries-old medium of stained glass. Working primarily with Charles Marq, master of the stained-glass studio Atelier Jacques Simon (now Atelier Simon-Marq) in Reims, France, Chagall executed 86 windows for churches, synagogues, and museums across Europe, Israel, and the United States. Stained glass was a perfect medium for the artist, allowing him to explore the effect of intense color on a monumental scale while updating ways of working with the traditional medium. As Marq stated in 1977, "Chagall is a real colorist, which doesn't just mean he uses blue, red, and green. There are people who use blue, red, and green who never become colorists. Chagall is a great colorist because he has a psychic sense of color inside himself."
