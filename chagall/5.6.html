CONSERVATION of the Windows

The deposits of oil and calcium carbonate appeared as a white film before treatment, most visible in raking light (light that is directed at an angle across the front surface) (1), though traces can also be seen in this detail near the figure's face in the theater panel with a combination of raking and transmitted light (light that is directed from the back) (2). Condensation caused putty components to wash gradually down the surface of the panels, producing drip marks and a gray haze near the bottom. Conservation treatment carefully removed these deposits, allowing the matting and the trace lines to be more apparent (3–4).
